SALES PLAYBOOK — COMMERCIAL ANALYTICS DIVISION
Version 3.2 | Owned by: Sales Operations | Last reviewed: January 2024

1. SALES METHODOLOGY OVERVIEW

Our sales motion is built on the MEDDPICC framework:
  M — Metrics         : quantify the economic impact for the buyer
  E — Economic Buyer  : identify and access the person who controls budget
  D — Decision Criteria: understand the formal scoring rubric
  D — Decision Process : map every approval step and timeline
  P — Paper Process   : legal, security, and procurement requirements
  I — Identify Pain   : connect our solution to a felt business problem
  C — Champion        : build and coach an internal advocate
  C — Competition     : know who else is in the deal and our differentiation

Every opportunity in Salesforce must have MEDDPICC fields completed before
moving from Stage 3 (Solution Presented) to Stage 4 (Proposal Sent).

2. QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

A qualified opportunity must satisfy all three of the following:

  a) BUDGET CONFIRMED
     The economic buyer has confirmed a budget range of at least USD 50,000
     annual contract value. Deals below this threshold are routed to inside sales.

  b) TIMELINE CONFIRMED
     The prospect has a stated go-live or decision date within 12 months.

  c) PROBLEM-SOLUTION FIT
     At least one of the following business pains must be documented:
       - Manual reporting consuming more than 20 hours per analyst per week
       - Inability to consolidate data from more than three source systems
       - Board-level reporting errors in the last two quarters
       - Compliance audit findings related to data lineage

3. DEAL STAGES AND EXIT CRITERIA

  Stage 1 — Prospecting: Entry: Lead created. Exit: Discovery call completed, pain identified.
  Stage 2 — Discovery: Entry: Pain confirmed. Exit: Technical requirements gathered.
  Stage 3 — Solution Presented: Entry: Demo delivered. Exit: MEDDPICC 80% complete.
  Stage 4 — Proposal Sent: Entry: Commercial proposal delivered. Exit: Verbal acceptance.
  Stage 5 — Negotiation: Entry: Redlines received. Exit: All terms agreed.
  Stage 6 — Closed Won or Closed Lost: Entry: Signature received or deal withdrawn.

Target stage velocity: S1 to S2 14 days, S2 to S3 21 days, S3 to S4 14 days,
S4 to S5 10 days, S5 to S6 7 days.

4. CLOSING TECHNIQUES

4.1 The Assumptive Close
Used when the prospect has given multiple positive signals. The rep assumes forward momentum.

4.2 The Summary Close
Used at Stage 5 to reduce hesitation by recapping agreed value and payback period.

4.3 The Competitive Close
Acknowledge competitor strength, pivot to differentiation, ask for decision criteria clarification.

4.4 The Limited-Time Close
Used only when there is a genuine verifiable time-bound incentive.
Do not fabricate urgency. Reps caught using false urgency will receive a formal warning.

5. DISCOVERY CALL STRUCTURE — 45 MINUTES

  00 to 05 min: Agenda setting and rapport
  05 to 15 min: Current state — how is the prospect solving the problem today
  15 to 25 min: Pain exploration — SPIN questioning
  25 to 35 min: Preliminary solution mapping
  35 to 40 min: Next steps with owners and dates
  40 to 45 min: Administrative — stakeholder list, technical contact, RFP timeline

Always send a call summary email within 2 hours.

7. ESCALATION POLICY

  Deal size above USD 500,000: requires VP sign-off on commercial terms
  Deal size above USD 1,000,000: requires CFO review before Stage 4
  Non-standard payment terms: requires Finance approval
  Contract term above 3 years: requires Legal and CFO approval
  Free pilot above 60 days: requires VP approval
  Custom SLA commitments: requires Head of Customer Success approval

All escalations submitted via Salesforce Escalation Request flow.
Target turnaround 2 business days for VP, 5 business days for CFO or Legal.
